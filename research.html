The Impact of AI Chatbot Use on Teen Loneliness and Emotional Well-Being

Primary Researcher: Aashiga Moorthy

Research Question

How does frequent interactions with anthropomorphic AI chatbots influence reported feelings of loneliness, emotional attachment, and real-world social engagement among high school adolescents?

Motivation

As large language models (LLMs) and conversational companions become ubiquitous, teenagers increasingly rely on synthetic agents for casual conversation, advice, and emotional support. Understanding the balance between immediate relief and potential long-term psychological displacement of peer connections is critical for ethical AI design.

Background

Current literature highlights both beneficial support structures provided by conversational agents and potential risks of parasocial dependence. This study examines these dynamics specifically within the high school demographic during peak developmental social stages.

Methodology

A mixed-methods approach combining qualitative survey analysis, standard psychological isolation metrics (e.g., UCLA Loneliness Scale adjustments), and structured analysis of user interaction patterns with conversational AI interfaces.

Current Progress

Phase: Data synthesis and qualitative framework compilation. Initial qualitative surveys are being categorized into interaction archetype profiles.

Future Work

Formulating algorithmic guidelines and interface design principles that encourage healthy conversational boundaries and promote real-world social connection.
